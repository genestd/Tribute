Hall of Fame
Pedro Martínez is a former Major League Baseball pitcher from the Dominican Republic. He played 18 seasons in the majors and was elected to the Hall of Fame in his first year of eligibility. He began his career with the Dodgers and Expos, but is most remembered for his tenure with the Boston Red Sox, winning the World Series in 2004. Pedro was anomalous not just for the statistics he produced, but for dominating the majors during the steriod era as a 5'11" 170 pound pitcher.
